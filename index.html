Zero-State Marketing Dashboard

Executive Business Case & Revenue Impact Analysis • US Market • May 2026

Bottom line: ~26% of US accounts created in the last 2 years (273K accounts) see an empty marketing analytics dashboard today — no sends, no contacts, no connected store/integration. These "zero-state" users convert to paid at 0.29% vs 5.33% for non-zero-state, log in 2.8× less often, and churn at a 37% higher rate.

Opportunity: Even modest improvements to zero-state activation (converting 5–15% of them out of zero-state) could unlock $580K–$2.6M in incremental annualized revenue from trial-to-paid conversion and retained MRR alone.

Recommendation: GO — Invest in zero-state onboarding improvements for Q3/Q4 FY26 with clear activation KPIs.

4. Why We Should Proceed: Data-Backed Rationale

Recommendation: Invest in Zero-State Activation

  1. Scale of opportunity: 273K accounts (26% of US 2yr cohort) currently derive no value from the analytics dashboard.
  2. Conversion gap: Non–zero-state accounts convert to paid at 18× the rate (5.33% vs 0.29%). Even partial activation could drive meaningful new bookings.
  3. Engagement gap: Login frequency is 12.7× higher for non–zero-state. More logins = more chances to discover value, run campaigns, and upgrade.
  4. Revenue concentration: Non–zero-state holds 99.6% of cohort MRR. Migrating even a fraction of zero-state users "over the line" has compounding MRR impact.
  5. Retention lift: Zero-state churn is 37% higher. Reducing zero-state population reduces churn from the base — a lever on Net Revenue Retention.

7. Key Insights & Conclusions

  1. Zero-state is a mass phenomenon, not an edge case. 26% of US accounts created in 2 years have an empty dashboard — that's 273K accounts with near-zero engagement or monetization.
  2. The conversion gap is the strongest signal. 18× difference in paid conversion rate (5.33% vs 0.29%) represents the single largest untapped booking opportunity in the early lifecycle funnel.
  3. Login frequency and product usage collapse in zero-state. 12.7× fewer login days means these users are not discovering product value. Improving "first data in" is the critical unlock.
  4. Churn risk is elevated but modest in absolute terms. 37% higher relative churn is meaningful at scale ($362K–$1.1M saved per year), especially as the base grows.
  5. ICPs respond differently. E-commerce / entertainment have lower zero-state rates (~7–8%) — likely due to natural store connections. B2B and unclassified accounts have 14–39% zero-state, suggesting they need different activation paths (e.g., contact import, form builder, manual CSV upload).
  6. High-value accounts are not affected. <0.5% of $299+ MRR accounts are zero-state. This is overwhelmingly a long-tail / early-lifecycle problem — and that's where growth comes from.
